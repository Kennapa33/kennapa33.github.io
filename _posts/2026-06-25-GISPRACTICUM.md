In pursuing my Undergraduate Geographic Information Science Certification at the University of Arizona, the final course I was required to take was a self-led practicum project. I had originally planned to make a habitat suitability model for Gila Topminnow (*𝘗𝘰𝘦𝘤𝘪𝘭𝘪𝘰𝘱𝘴𝘪𝘴 𝘰𝘤𝘤𝘪𝘥𝘦𝘯𝘵𝘢𝘭𝘪𝘴 𝘰𝘤𝘤𝘪𝘥𝘦𝘯𝘵𝘢𝘭𝘪𝘴*) in the Santa Cruz River. After initial preliminary research and discussion with my GIS mentor, I found that my initial idea was too small scale. There unfortunately was just not enough publicly available research material focused on this species in the Santa Cruz river, despite its return just making headlines a few years prior. Instead I had to broaden my scope, and I chose to look at predator distribution and mean river discharges over the past five years in major subbasins connected to the Gila River system.
